# Title


I was looking for my next journey into malware analysis and this time around I decided to take a look at QuasarRat. It is a .net based RAT that is a bit more complicated than AsyncRAT and seemed to have been favored by Advanced Persistent Threats (APT) based out of china for awhile. According to an article posted by [Huntress](https://www.huntress.com/threat-library/threat-actors/apt10) parts of the APT (APT10) group were indicted in 2018. This made me interested in why or who is still using it today and possibly exposing their infrastructure, lets see what we can find out.
